Manitoba Mathematical Contest

Wednesday, February 25, 2026

Proudly hosted by Robert Craigen and the University of Manitoba Mathematics Department.

The Manitoba Mathematical Contest, or MMC, is a competition for high school students in Manitoba. It aims to promote mathematical problem-solving skills and encourage interest in mathematics among students. It runs every year in February, concurrent with the Pascal, Cayley, and Fermat competitions.

Contest Outline

The MMC consists of 10 questions. Each question is worth 5 points and the total score is out of 50 points. The contest takes 2 hours, starting at 9:00 AM and ending at 11:00 AM. A procter is required to monitor the contest, and the following are prohibited: calculators of any kind, straight edges, compasses, protractors, or any other electronic devices.


The contest is designed to be challenging and to test the problem-solving skills of the participants. The questions are not meant to be solved by rote memorization or by using standard formulas, but rather by applying mathematical concepts and reasoning to solve problems. The contest is open to all high school students in Manitoba, and it is a great opportunity for students to challenge themselves and to develop their mathematical skills. It can only be taken once in high school but may be done unofficially for fun or as a mathematical challenge.


All work must be shown to obtain full marks. The first 5 questions consist of parts 'a' and 'b' and questions 6-10 are single-part questions. All questions must be answered in the space provided but extra room is available on the back of each page as well as pages 23 and 24. All questions are designed to test mathematical reasoning and problem-solving skills as well as critical thinking ability.



Contest

Past Contests

The Manitoba Mathematical Contest has been held annually since 1957. The contest has a rich history and has seen many talented students participate over the years. The contest has evolved over time, with changes to the format and the types of questions asked. However, the core mission of the contest remains the same: to promote mathematical problem-solving skills and to encourage interest in mathematics among high school students in Manitoba.

Awards

Awards

The Manitoba Mathematical Contest recognizes the top performers in the contest with awards and scholarships. The awards are based on the total score achieved by the participants, and they are typically given out at an awards ceremony held after the contest. The awards may include cash prizes, scholarships, and certificates of achievement. The top performers in the contest may also be invited to participate in other mathematics competitions at the regional, national, or international level.
